## Exercise
## Defining measures of spread

## Instructions
## - Match each definition to the correct measure of spread.
## - Classify each statement under:
##   Range, Variance, or Standard Deviation.

## MY SOLUTION:

## Range
## - This measure shows how spread out the data is
##   between the smallest and largest values.
## - This measure is calculated by finding the difference
##   between the minimum and maximum values of a variable.

## Variance
## - To calculate this measure, square the distances of
##   each data point from the mean and add up the results.
## - This measure uses squared units of a variable,
##   so it is generally used as an intermediate step
##   when calculating the standard deviation.

## Standard Deviation
## - This measure uses the variable's original units
##   to show the distribution of data points from the mean.
## - This measure is found by taking the square root
##   of the sum of squared distances from each data point to the mean.
